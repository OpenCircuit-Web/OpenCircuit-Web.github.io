What is OpenCircuit Conference?

This website is a template that you could use for a (small) academic conference. It’s got a page for registration, where you can embed a Google Form or some other way for participants to register. It’s got a program page to list the schedule for the conference. It’s got a directions page to add a bunch of instructions how to get to the conference. It’s got a page to put up the flyers and other promotional materials for folks to download.

You can use this template by cloning all the files from this GitHub repo here. This template was created from a website I made for MathConnections 2019, in case you want to see an example of this template being used in the wild.

What does OpenCircuit Need?

Submission Tracks and Guidelines:

Poster and In-progress Paper (2 pages, excluding references, ACM conference template):

Preliminary or In-progress Research Snapshots: Capturing early-stage research that introduces novel ideas or methodologies in the field.

Experience Reports: Insights into the challenges and successes encountered in the construction or deployment of open-source accelerators, emphasizing the tools and processes utilized.

Special Issue and Invited Paper (6-8 pages, excluding references, ACM conference template):

Experience Reports: Detailed accounts of the challenges and triumphs in building or deploying open-source accelerators, offering a deep dive into the tools and methodologies employed. Essays and Surveys: Engaging and thought-provoking discussions, either supporting or critiquing the methodologies, practices, or general approaches within open-source reconfigurable computing. Reflective Retrospectives: In-depth reflections on past projects, tools, languages, and techniques that have significantly influenced the accelerator design landscape. Latest Progress and Novel Solutions: Updates on the most recent advancements and innovative solutions addressing the outlined topics.
